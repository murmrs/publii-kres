Mecha-board

Steven Kreschollek October 2, 2020

My first real introduction to mechanical keyboards, outside of maybe using them a few times in school at old computer labs, was when I was morking my first job at Walgreens. Even though it was in the 2000’s, Walgreens utilized a POS system powered by IBM’s AS/400 (they may even still; it’s been a hot minute since I was there). The system in our store had a wonderfully obnoxious Model-M variant attached to it’s console.

While I had familiary with high-quality (for the time) gaming perpherals, I wasn’t super well versed with mechanical keyboards themselves. Time passed, and when my Razer products (another post for another time) all started giving out, I looked at the market and settled on a Corsair K60 as my first truly mechanical keyboard (though the function keys were still membrane).

It worked well, until it didn’t.

Keys started giving out, and I needed to look at different options.

While my Wife and I were dating, I explored other, smaller options, eventually settling on a Poker II with clear switches. That keyboard lasted a long time - staying with me, and finding it’s place as my ‘office’ keyboard of choice at my previous agency.

My brother still uses that keyboard - it’s built like a tank, and will probably outlast both of us.

After I left that agency, I decided I wanted a bit of a better setup at home. I ended up ordering the ErgoDox Infinity from Massdrop (now Drop) with Cherry Blue’s. I’m sure it annoys everone on the Zoom calls we all constantly find oursleves on these days - but I love it. It took some getting used to, but the value in a split keyboard to me is tremendous. It feels better. It works better. And I can program the ever livin’ heck out of it.

There are many keyboards like it, but this one is mine.

But I don’t think it’ll be the ‘Endgame (trademark pending, or something)’. The HHKB has been on my list for more years than I care to admit, and while my wallet continunes to say no, my eyes are saying yes.
